## Visual Generation Rules

- Do not reuse the exact same color palette across all locales.
- Keep a dark global brand base, but localize accent temperature, title contrast, subline softness and bullet visibility.
- `Background base` is the main canvas color.
- `Background secondary` is used for gradients, cards, panels, mockup shadows or subtle depth.
- `Accent color` is used for highlights, thin lines, active UI cues, before/after arrows, small glow zones and key bullets.
- `Title color` must be used for the main headline only.
- `Subline color` must be used for the subtitle or supporting line only.
- `Bullet color` must be used for bullets, small proof points, checkmarks and short trust markers.
- Do not place bright panels behind title text.
- Do not use large saturated red glows behind the headline.
- Do not create dense patterns behind copy.
- Keep the copy zone stable, dark and readable.
- The first screenshot should sell the problem and transformation, not settings.
- The visual sequence should follow: problem -> simple flow -> control -> privacy.
- The global trust proof should remain clear: local processing, no accounts and no uploads.
- Avoid any claim of AI generation, automatic templates, DRM removal, full EPUB editing or affiliation with reading platforms.
